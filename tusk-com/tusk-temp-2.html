Tom Thumb is a popular folk tale of European origin that has been told and retold in various versions over the centuries. The story centers on a tiny boy, named Tom Thumb due to his small size, who was born into a poor family with many children. Despite his size, Tom Thumb is very clever and brave. One day, due to their extreme poverty, the parents decide to abandon their children in the forest. However, Tom Thumb, who had overheard their plans, drops bread crumbs to mark the way back home. Unfortunately, the birds eat the crumbs, and the children get lost.

Tom Thumb and his brothers find shelter in a house that turns out to belong to an ogre. The ogre plans to eat them, but Tom Thumb, using his wits, swaps their caps for the crowns of the ogre's daughters while they sleep. This causes the ogre to mistake his daughters for the children, and he ends up eating them instead of Tom Thumb and his brothers. Then, Tom Thumb leads his brothers out of the ogre's house, and on the way, he steals the ogre's seven-league boots, which allow him to travel great distances in a short time.

With the seven-league boots, Tom Thumb becomes a royal messenger and earns enough money to return home and rescue his family from poverty. Thanks to his bravery and ingenuity, Tom Thumb proves that size does not matter when it comes to overcoming adversity and protecting loved ones. The story of Tom Thumb highlights values such as intelligence, courage, and perseverance in the face of hardship.
